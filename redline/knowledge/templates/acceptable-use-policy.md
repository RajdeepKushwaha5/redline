## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of organizational technology resources including computers, networks, email, internet, mobile devices, and cloud services. It protects the organization and its stakeholders from security risks arising from misuse.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and any individual granted access to organizational technology resources. It covers all company-owned, leased, or licensed hardware, software, and services, as well as personal devices used to access company resources (BYOD).

## 3. General Use

- Technology resources are provided primarily for business purposes
- Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work, violate any policy, or consume excessive resources
- Users are responsible for the security of their credentials and must not share passwords or access tokens
- All data created, stored, or transmitted on company systems is the property of the organization

## 4. Device Policy

### 4.1 Company-Owned Devices
- Must have endpoint protection software installed and up to date
- Must use full-disk encryption
- Must have automatic screen lock enabled (maximum 5 minutes of inactivity)
- Must not have unauthorized software installed
- Must be returned upon termination of employment

### 4.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)
- Personal devices may be used to access company resources only when enrolled in the Mobile Device Management (MDM) system
- Minimum requirements: current OS version, screen lock enabled, encryption enabled
- The organization reserves the right to remotely wipe company data from personal devices
- Personal devices must be reported lost or stolen within 4 hours

## 5. Network and Internet Use

- Internet access is provided for business purposes and limited personal use
- The organization monitors network traffic for security purposes
- Prohibited activities: accessing illegal content, peer-to-peer file sharing of copyrighted material, bypassing security controls (proxy avoidance, VPN to circumvent filters), cryptocurrency mining on company resources
- Use of public Wi-Fi to access company resources requires VPN

## 6. Email and Communication

- Email must not be used to transmit classified or restricted data without encryption
- Users must verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive information
- Phishing or suspicious emails must be reported to the security team immediately
- Auto-forwarding of company email to personal accounts is prohibited

## 7. Cloud Services and Software

- Only approved cloud services may be used for company data (shadow IT is prohibited)
- Software installation requires approval from IT
- SaaS account provisioning must go through the IT request process
- Credentials for cloud services must use SSO where available, otherwise unique passwords with MFA

## 8. Data Handling

- Data must be handled according to the data classification policy
- Restricted and confidential data must not be stored on unapproved personal devices or cloud services
- USB drives and removable media are prohibited for transferring restricted data unless encrypted and pre-approved
- Data must be disposed of according to the data retention and disposal policy

## 9. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly prohibited:
- Unauthorized access or attempts to access systems, data, or accounts not assigned to the user
- Installing or running malicious software, hacking tools, or network scanning tools without authorization
- Harassing, threatening, or discriminatory communications
- Sharing company credentials, tokens, or access keys with unauthorized individuals
- Circumventing or disabling security controls, monitoring tools, or audit mechanisms
- Using company resources for unauthorized commercial activities
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential or proprietary information

## 10. Monitoring and Enforcement

- The organization monitors technology resource usage for security, compliance, and operational purposes
- Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company resources
- Monitoring includes but is not limited to: network traffic, email metadata, endpoint activity, access logs
- Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and legal action

## 11. Reporting

Users must report the following to the security team immediately:
- Suspected security incidents or data breaches
- Lost or stolen devices
- Phishing attempts or suspicious communications
- Unauthorized access or policy violations observed

## 12. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually or when significant changes in technology or risk landscape occur. Updates follow the organization's policy change management process.
